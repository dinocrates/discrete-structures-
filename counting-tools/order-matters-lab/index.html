Order Matters Lab

Define the Outcome Before Counting. Select objects, decide whether positions matter, and inspect how one group becomes one outcome or many role assignments.

Guided Investigations

1. Team or Roles?

Switch the same three people between a team and role assignments.

ObservationOne team expands into 3! = 6 assignments when positions acquire meaning.

2. Explain the Quotient

Why does 336 / 6 equal 56?

ObservationEvery group has exactly six internal orders.

3. Rank Alerts

Swap Priority 1 and Priority 2 without changing the selected alerts.

ObservationThe set stays fixed, but the ranking changes.

4. Feature Set

Preview several internal orders of the same four features.

ObservationSelection order does not change which features are enabled.

5. Failover Sequence

Compare the same services in opposite failover positions.

ObservationFirst, second, third, and fourth positions determine behavior.
